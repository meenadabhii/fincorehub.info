Avoid these common mistakes in your 20s/30s

1 Living Beyond Means

Overspending on luxuries (fancy cars, eating out) without saving/investing leads to debt cycles. Stick to a 50/30/20 budget.

Tip: Use apps like Mint to track spending.

2 Ignoring Emergency Funds

78% of millennials can't cover a $1,000 emergency. Aim for 3–6 months' expenses in a high-yield savings account.

Tip: Automate savings with direct deposits.

3 The Credit Score Horror Story

"I missed just one $35 credit card payment at 23. Seven years later, that single mistake still haunted me when I got denied for a mortgage rate that would've saved me $87,000."

Payment history makes up 35% of your credit score. Even small mistakes can linger for years.

Tip: Set up autopay for minimum payments at least.

5 The Insurance Trap

"I saved $15/month by skipping renter's insurance... until my $3,000 laptop was stolen from my apartment."

Being underinsured is one of the most expensive "savings" strategies. Review coverage annually.

Tip: Bundle insurance policies for better rates.
